The stray cat colonies of Andalusia

Friday, 4 January 2019 • Mariska Grobler

A couple of months after moving into a finca in the campo, I felt sorry for a pregnant mother cat and her four kittens who eventually emerged from the olive grove. She appeared well before them and all the more frequently when I started leaving food out for her. Today, the four kittens, one male and three females, are now semi-domesticated due to our care. The mother remains unapproachable but still turns up daily for food, along with her gang of other stray cats, around four of them - possibly her own kittens from previous litters.

For some people they’re vulnerable animals in need of protection. For others of course, they’re pests that need to be eradicated, or worse, ignored.

Why don’t the Spanish neuter or spay their cats as a matter of obvious choice? When kittens are born, they are either drowned or turned out. For the arguably unlucky survivors it’s a hard life and only the fittest, healthiest, most cunning, survive. But even these ones don’t seem to live very long.

Most feral cats are born in the wild and never experience human companionship during their critical period of socialization (about 2-9 weeks of age), so they will always remain frightened of people. Only very young feral kittens can learn to adjust to living in a home. Feral cats left to fend for themselves isn't a great option. Untended street cats face death by starvation, disease, inclement weather, traffic and predatory animals.

Experts estimate that 60 to 70 percent of wild-born kittens die in their first 6 months. Feral cats that do survive kitten-hood then spend the rest of their lives struggling to find food and repeatedly breeding... adding more homeless kittens to the growing population. Forming cat colonies is one of their survival tools.

There are no simple solutions. Some people try to help feral cats by leaving dishes of food, but unfortunately, feeding alone of course does not solve any problems. Extra food usually brings more cats into the area...leading to fighting, infectious disease transmission, and lots of homeless kittens.

Griffin, one of the stray kittens we have domesticated


What is the solution?

Some humane groups have begun to use another strategy for dealing with feral cats. This method involves capturing, neutering, and vaccinating feral cats and then returning them to their former territory where they rejoin their existing cat colonies. Caretakers provide food and shelters to protect the cats in the colony from wind and weather.

Backers of this method say it can effectively control feral populations because the neutered cats no longer reproduce but they still continue to defend their territory against incoming cats.

There are a few charities in Andalusia that try to help these cats:

  • Animal Care España

    ACE stands for 'Animal Care Espana', Spanish Hounds In Need, in short SHIN. In Holland we use the name ACE. In Belgium our organization is known as SHIN. Both countries use another logo but represent the same organization. ACE | SHIN fights against the abuse and killing of neglected and abused (stray)dogs in Southern Spain.

  • Adana

    ADANA (Association for the Rights of Abandoned Animals) is a charitable organisation on the Costa del Sol run by volunteers. Our aims are to promote love and affection for dogs, to provide facilities for the shelter and care of abandoned, sick and injured dogs, whilst seeking new homes for them and to assist the local authorities with such animals. ADANA never puts down a healthy animal.

  • F.A.M.A Spain

    F.A.M.A. is a foundation that is funded solely by voluntary contributions to provide an acceptable standard of life to abandoned animals while they are in our care. F.A.M.As policy is never to have a healthy animal put to sleep.

  • PAD - Protección de Animales Domésticos

    Protección de Animales in Mijas Costa Malaga, is a registered charity that shelters and actively finds new homes for abandoned cats and dogs.

  • SOS Animals

    SOS (Spay Our Strays) was founded on the Costa del Sol in 1997 and gained official charitable status two years later. Our mission is simple – to find loving homes for the dogs in our care. And our pledge is to provide each animal with a safe and affectionate environment and a secure home with us for ever, if need be.

  • Triple A Marbella

    We are an officially registered non-profit animal charity association with number 02604 in Registro de Asociaciones de Junta de Andalucía. Triple A is mostly run by volunteers, dedicated to help and care for the abandoned and abused animals on the Costa del Sol in the Marbella and San Pedro Alcántara area.

  • A.I.D. Animals In Distress

    A.I.D. is a registered charity in Andalucia dedicated to finding homes for stray, abandoned, abused animals found in Coin and surrounding villages in this part of Malaga in Andalucia. All the dogs that we rescue and take care off, are located in our shelter and all the young puppies are in foster care. We also have a small cattery.

  • Axarquia Animal Rescue

    Axarquia Animal Rescue is run by unpaid volunteers. We rely totally on our supporters for funding and help to carry out the many day to day activities. We would be unable to do what we do without help and support from people like you.

  • Valle Verde Animal Rescue

    What we do:

    • Remove animals to safety from the street or situations of neglect and abuse
    • Offer animals immediate shelter, medical attention, food and love
    • Bring pets and owners together for a lifetime of love and friendship through our adoption programme
    • Educate the public and work towards changing attitudes towards animals and cruelty to animals

Get in touch to share your own cat stories. What do you recommend to help solve the issue? How can we help Spain to improve on their outlook and treatment of animals?

helpmekaar.org

Die Helpmekaar Plan is beplan vir daardie middelklasgroep in ons gemeenskap wat nie kan reken op sosio-ekonomiese hulp van die regering of NGO’s tydens die depressie wat voorlê nie. Ons debietorders hop. Ons verloor ons werk. Ons besighede maak toe. Ons rekenings is agter. Ons versekering verval. Ons verbande en voertuie word nie betaal nie en sal mettertyd deur finansiers teruggeneem word vir ‘n appel en ‘n ei. Baie van ons gaan binnekort nie meer kos hê vir onsself en ons kinders nie. Sonder dat dit sigbaar is na buite, gaan ons mekaar help; gaan ons saam, baie hard baklei vir ons oorlewing. Geen welsyn of liefdadigheid nie maar wedersydse lojaliteit en hulp. Binne ons groep help ons mekaar en baklei ons saam vir oorlewing. Sonder die groep staan u alleen en sal u waarskynlik ondergaan. Daarom word u en u familie en vriende hartlik uitgenooi om by ons groep in te skakel. Hoe meer ons is, hoe sterker sal ons wees! Saam gaan ons met trots en waardigheid anderkant uitkom!
